Pitfalls

The risks are real. The field is moving faster than policy.

⛔ FABRICATION & HALLUCINATION
LLMs confidently fabricate citations, invent statistics, and hallucinate author names. In biomedical literature this causes direct patient harm if unchecked.
"Fabricated citations were found in 47% of AI-generated literature sections when unverified."
✓ Rule: Verify every citation, every statistic. Always.
📝 AUTHORSHIP INTEGRITY
ICMJE: AI cannot be an author. But undisclosed AI use is now widespread. Journals are racing to develop disclosure standards.
✓ Rule: Disclose AI use in methods. Authors are accountable for all content.
💬 HOMOGENIZATION OF SCIENCE
When everyone uses the same AI tools trained on the same corpora, scientific writing converges. Novel ideas get smoothed into familiar framings. AI sounds confident even when wrong.
✓ Rule: Use AI as a tool, not a ghostwriter. Your ideas first.
🕵️ PEER REVIEW GAMING
AI can write convincing-sounding peer reviews. Predatory journals are using AI to rubber-stamp submissions. COPE guidelines are evolving but enforcement is patchy.
✓ Rule: Human accountability is non-negotiable.
Bottom line: AI is a force multiplier for research productivity. Its risks are manageable with transparency, citation verification, and institutional policy. The field that adapts thoughtfully will lead.
Practical Skills

Structure your prompt. Transform your manuscript output.

✅ EFFECTIVE RESEARCH PROMPT
You are an expert cardiothoracic surgery researcher.
# Task Write a structured abstract for a retrospective study on robotic mitral repair outcomes.
# Data [paste your results table here]
# Format JTCVS structured abstract: Objective / Methods / Results / Conclusions. 250 words max.
# Constraints Do not fabricate statistics. Use only what I provide.
❌ WEAK PROMPT — INVITES HALLUCINATION
"Write an abstract about my mitral repair study."
↳ AI has no data → invents statistics
↳ No format constraint → wrong structure for JTCVS
↳ No role context → generic, not cardiothoracic-specific
↳ No output guardrails → hallucinated citations likely
