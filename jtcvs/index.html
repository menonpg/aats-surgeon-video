Practical Skills

Structure your prompt. Transform your manuscript output.

✅ EFFECTIVE RESEARCH PROMPT
You are an expert cardiothoracic surgery researcher.
# Task Write a structured abstract for a retrospective study on robotic mitral repair outcomes.
# Data [paste your actual results table here]
# Format JTCVS structured abstract: Objective / Methods / Results / Conclusions. 250 words max.
# Constraints Use ONLY the data I provide. Do not fabricate statistics.
❌ WEAK PROMPT — INVITES HALLUCINATION
"Write an abstract about my mitral repair study."
↳ No data provided → AI invents statistics
↳ No format → wrong structure for JTCVS
↳ No role → generic, not CT-surgery specific
↳ No guardrails → hallucinated citations likely
🎭
1. Role
"You are an expert CT surgeon and researcher..."
📋
2. Task
Specific, verifiable goal — not "help me write"
📊
3. Data
Paste your actual results — never let AI guess
📐
4. Format
JTCVS structured abstract · word limits · sections
🔒
5. Constraints
"Only use data I provide. No fabrication."
💡 Chain-of-Thought tip: Add "Think step by step before writing" — dramatically improves logical consistency in methods sections and discussion reasoning. Try it at learn.aatsqualitygateway.org

Pitfalls

The risks are real. The field is moving faster than policy.

⛔ FABRICATION & HALLUCINATION
LLMs confidently fabricate citations, invent statistics, and hallucinate author names. In biomedical literature this causes direct patient harm if unchecked — wrong dosing, false efficacy claims, invented safety data.
"Fabricated citations were found in 47% of AI-generated literature sections when unverified."
✓ Rule: Verify every citation. Every statistic. Always.
📝 AUTHORSHIP INTEGRITY
ICMJE: AI cannot be an author. But undisclosed AI use is now widespread. Nature, NEJM, JAMA, and JTCVS are all developing disclosure frameworks.
✓ Rule: Disclose AI use in methods. Authors are fully accountable.
💬 HOMOGENIZATION OF SCIENCE
When everyone uses the same AI tools trained on the same corpora, scientific writing converges. Novel hypotheses get smoothed into familiar framings. AI sounds confident even when wrong. The "academic voice" is disappearing.
✓ Rule: Use AI as a tool, not a ghostwriter. Your ideas first.
🕵️ PEER REVIEW GAMING
AI can write convincing-sounding peer reviews indistinguishable from human ones. Predatory journals are using AI to rubber-stamp submissions. COPE guidelines are evolving — but enforcement is patchy.
✓ Rule: Human accountability is non-negotiable.
Bottom line: AI is a force multiplier for research productivity. Its risks are manageable with transparency, verification, and journal policy. The field that adapts thoughtfully will lead.
